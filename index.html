about us

Situated in the centre of town, right in the busiest part of the main street Shirok Sokak, with a view to the Officers’ Home as a cultural-historical monument, оnly 15 kilometers from Baba mountain with the Pelister peak, famous for the unique Macedonian Molika pine аnd only 2 kilometers from the Ancient Macedonian city of Heraclea Linkestis, to which there is also a footpath. Station44 Аpartments have the ideal position for reaching the most important facilities that the town offers: shops, Centre of Culture, Theater, cinema, restaurants, bars, bakeries, sweets shops, car park, taxi station, and the pedestrian zone – the Promenade.

Bitola, often referred to as the "City of Consuls," stands as one of the most charming and historically rich cities in North Macedonia. The city has a unique blend of Ottoman, Balkan, and European influences, evident in its architecture, traditions, and culture. Shirok Sokak, the vibrant pedestrian street, is the heart of the city’s social life, where locals and tourists alike enjoy its many cafes, restaurants, and boutiques while strolling past elegant neoclassical buildings. This lively street is also a hub for festivals, live music, and cultural events throughout the year.

For history enthusiasts, Bitola offers a treasure trove of landmarks. The Clock Tower, a historic symbol of the city, and the Yeni Mosque, an architectural masterpiece from the Ottoman era, are must-visit sites. The city’s rich consular past is preserved in several buildings that once housed foreign embassies, reflecting its significance as a diplomatic center during the Ottoman Empire.

Nature lovers will find Bitola equally captivating. The nearby Pelister National Park, the oldest in the country, boasts spectacular natural beauty with its dense Molika pine forests, crystal-clear rivers, and serene mountain lakes. The park is a paradise for hikers, offering trails that lead to stunning viewpoints and the iconic Pelister Eyes—two glacial lakes nestled high in the mountains. In winter, Pelister transforms into a snowy wonderland, attracting skiing and snowboarding enthusiasts.

No visit to Bitola would be complete without exploring the ancient city of Heraclea Lyncestis. Founded by Philip II of Macedon, this archaeological site is renowned for its well-preserved Roman mosaics, amphitheater, and baths, offering a fascinating glimpse into the city’s classical past.

The city is also a cultural hotspot, hosting the Manaki Brothers International Cinematographers’ Film Festival, one of the oldest film festivals in the world. Visitors can explore the city’s museums, including the Museum of Bitola, which is housed in a former military academy attended by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, the founder of modern Turkey.

Bitola’s warm hospitality, rich history, and scenic surroundings make it an unforgettable destination for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure.
